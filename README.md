## Articles

High-impact articles and journalism discussing the practical and philosophical implications of AI in an African context.

* [**AI Ethics in Africa: Balancing Innovation and Rights**](https://www.africanleadershipmagazine.co.uk/ai-ethics-in-africa-balancing-innovation-and-rights/) - An overview of the challenges and opportunities, highlighting the need to anchor AI development in human rights and indigenous wisdom.
* [**Ethical AI Development in Kenya: The Role of Ethical Data Sourcing and Governance**](https://cipit.strathmore.edu/ethical-ai-development-in-kenya-the-role-of-ethical-data-sourcing-and-governance/) - A critical look at data exploitation, labor, and the need for legal frameworks in Kenya's AI ecosystem.
* [**What does it mean to decolonise AI?**](https://www.deeplearning.ai/the-batch/what-does-it-mean-to-decolonise-ai/) - An article exploring how AI systems can perpetuate colonial-era biases and the movement to build new, more equitable systems.
* [**The ethics of artificial intelligence, UNESCO and the African Ubuntu perspective**](https://www.emerald.com/insight/content/doi/10.1108/JICES-04-2022-0037/full/html) - Discusses how the African philosophy of *Ubuntu* (communalism, "I am because we are") can inform a more relational and humane approach to AI ethics.

## Research Papers

Academic papers and reports from institutions and scholars specializing in African AI ethics.

* [**Ethics of AI in Africa: Interrogating the role of Ubuntu and AI governance initiatives**](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/391808657_Ethics_of_AI_in_Africa_Interrogating_the_role_of_Ubuntu_and_AI_governance_initiatives) - A scholarly article that critically examines the application of *Ubuntu* philosophy to emerging AI governance frameworks in Africa.
* [**Legal and Ethical Implications of Data Privacy in Artificial Intelligence: A Review of Data Privacy Among Learners in Kenyan Secondary Schools**](https://journals.unesco.go.ke/index.php/jknatcom/article/view/170) - A paper focusing on the specific risks and ethical challenges of AI deployment in Kenya's education sector.
* [**Ethical Guidelines for Artificial Intelligence in Crime Control and Public Order Management in Kenya**](https://www.ssrc.org/wp-content/uploads/2024/06/APN_Briefing-Note_No.-41_061224_2.pdf) - A policy brief from the Social Science Research Council on the need for culturally-sensitive AI guidelines in Kenyan law enforcement.
* [**AI, Ethics and Policy Governance in Africa (Special Collection)**](https://www.cambridge.org/core/journals/data-and-policy/special-collections/ai-ethics-and-policy-governance-in-africa) - A special collection of papers from Cambridge University Press addressing data governance and policy innovations from diverse African perspectives.

* [**Catherine Muraga**](https://www.linkedin.com/in/catherine-muraga-18b7661b/) - Managing Director of the Microsoft Africa Development Center (ADC) in Nairobi, Kenya. A key industry voice on technology, innovation, and skills development in the region.

## Local Policies

Official strategy documents and policy frameworks from national governments and continental bodies.

* [**African Union: The Continental AI Strategy for Africa (2024)**](https://au.int/sites/default/files/documents/44004-doc-EN-_Continental_AI_Strategy_July_2024.pdf) - [PDF] The landmark framework adopted by the AU to guide the development and governance of AI across the continent, emphasizing inclusive, development-oriented, and ethical principles.
* [**Kenya: National AI Strategy (2025-2030)**](https://globaltaxnews.ey.com/news/2025-2193-kenya-releases-the-national-ai-strategy-2025-2030) - (Summary) An overview of Kenya's strategy, which focuses on digital infrastructure, data, research, and innovation to position the nation as a regional AI hub.
* [**South Africa: National AI Policy Framework (2024)**](https://www.policyvault.africa/policy/south-africa-national-artificial-intelligence-ai-policy-framework-2024/) - (Summary) Details on South Africa's framework, which aims to drive economic growth and societal well-being while fostering a robust AI ecosystem.
* [**Rwanda: National AI Policy (2023)**](https://www.ictworks.org/wp-content/uploads/2023/12/Rwanda_Artificial_Intelligence_Policy.pdf) - [PDF] Rwanda's official policy document, which aims to leverage AI for sustainable growth and position the country as a global innovator for responsible AI.
* [**Nigeria: National Artificial Intelligence Strategy (2025)**](https://ncair.nitda.gov.ng/wp-content/uploads/2025/09/National-Artificial-Intelligence-Strategy-19092025.pdf) - [PDF] Nigeria's official strategy, a collaborative effort to develop an inclusive and collaborative policy for AI development.
* [**Ghana: National AI Strategy (2023-2023)**](https://dig.watch/resource/ghanas-national-artificial-intelligence-strategy-2023-2033/) - (Summary) A summary of Ghana's 10-year plan to harness AI for inclusive and sustainable socioeconomic development.

## Relevant Books

Essential books and edited volumes that provide deep philosophical and practical insights into AI ethics in Africa.

* [**Responsible AI in Africa: Challenges and Opportunities (2023)**](httpshttps://library.oapen.org/handle/20.500.12657/60787) - (Open Access) Edited by Damian Okaibedi Eke, Kutoma Wakunuma, and Simisola Akintoye. This book brings together African scholars to discuss AI ethics from theoretical and practical African perspectives.
* [**AI Ethics in Higher Education: Insights from Africa and Beyond (2023)**](https://library.oapen.org/handle/20.500.12657/61297) - (Open Access) Edited by Caitlin C. Corrigan, et al. This book tackles the problem of integrating AI ethics into computer science and engineering curricula in Africa.
* [**AI in and for Africa: A Humanistic Perspective (2023)**](https://www.routledge.com/AI-in-and-for-Africa-A-Humanistic-Perspective/Brokensha-Kotze-Senekal/p/book/9781032215754) - By Susan Brokensha, et al. This book explores AI's intersection with Africa's socio-economic realities and advocates for a decolonial, humanistic approach.
